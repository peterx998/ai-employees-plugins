# Advertising Claims and Compliance Policy

> **Applies to:** ALL plugins, ALL agents, ALL workflows that generate, review, or publish advertising content. This is a global policy.

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-02  
**Owner:** Marketing Compliance / Legal Team  
**Review Cadence:** Quarterly or upon platform policy change  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines what advertising claims are prohibited, the platform-specific rules agents must follow when generating ad content, and the disclosure requirements for endorsements and influencer content. It ensures all agent-generated advertising complies with FTC, ASA, Meta, and TikTok regulations.

---

## 2. Prohibited Claims

### 2.1 Medical Efficacy Claims

Agents must **never** generate advertising content that claims a product:

| Prohibited Claim | Example |
|---|---|
| Cures a disease or condition | "Cures eczema in 3 days" |
| Treats a medical condition | "Clinically proven to treat psoriasis" |
| Diagnoses a condition | "Detects early signs of skin cancer" |
| Prevents illness | "Prevents bacterial infections" |
| Has pharmaceutical effects | "Works like a steroid without side effects" |
| Replaces medical treatment | "Better than prescription treatments" |

### 2.2 Guaranteed Results

| Prohibited Claim | Example |
|---|---|
| Money-back guarantee (without actual policy) | "Guaranteed results or triple your money back" |
| Specific timeframe promises | "Clear skin in exactly 7 days" |
| Universal effectiveness | "Works for 100% of users" |
| Permanent results | "Permanently eliminates breakouts" |
| Absolute language | "Never breaks out again" |

### 2.3 Before/After Without Disclosure

Before/after imagery in advertising is **prohibited** unless ALL of the following are met:

1. Images are genuine and unaltered (no digital enhancement of results)
2. The disclosure **"Individual results may vary. Results not guaranteed."** is clearly visible
3. The imagery has been reviewed and approved by the compliance team
4. The ad platform's specific before/after requirements are met (see §3)

### 2.4 Other Prohibited Claims

| Category | Prohibited | Acceptable Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| **Weight loss** | "Lose 10 pounds in a week" | "Supports a healthy lifestyle" |
| **Anti-aging** | "Erases wrinkles permanently" | "Helps reduce the appearance of fine lines" |
| **Natural/organic** | "100% natural with no chemicals" | "Formulated with naturally derived ingredients" |
| **Comparative** | "Better than [competitor brand]" | "Compare our ingredients to leading brands" |
| **Endorsement** | "Doctors recommend our product" | "Dermatologist-tested" (with substantiation) |
| **Scarcity** | "Only 2 left in stock!" (when false) | "Limited quantity available" (when true) |

## 3. Platform-Specific Rules

### 3.1 Meta Advertising Policies (Facebook / Instagram)

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **No deceptive claims** | Ads must not contain deceptive, false, or misleading claims |
| **No unrealistic expectations** | Ads must not promise unrealistic results |
| **Personal attributes** | Ads must not assert or imply personal attributes (e.g., "Do you have acne?" as ad targeting text) |
| **Before/after** | Restricted; requires clear disclaimer, may require additional review |
| **Body image** | Ads must not depict unrealistic body standards or body-shame |
| **Health products** | Must include: "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." |
| **Misleading urgency** | "Limited time offer" must be genuine; false countdowns are prohibited |
| **Testimonials** | Must reflect genuine, typical experience; non-typical results must be disclosed |

**Required Meta Disclaimers for Health/Beauty Ads:**
```
"This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
"Individual results may vary. Results not guaranteed."
```

### 3.2 TikTok Ad Guidelines

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **No health claims without substantiation** | All health-related claims require scientific substantiation on file |
| **No exaggerated claims** | "Miracle," "breakthrough," "instant," "overnight" are prohibited |
| **Before/after** | Restricted; requires platform pre-approval |
| **Weight loss products** | Additional restrictions; must not target users under 18; no unrealistic body imagery |
| **Influencer content** | Must use paid partnership tag + #ad disclosure |
| **Misleading editing** | Ads must not use deceptive editing to exaggerate results |
| **Minors** | Health/wellness ads must not target users under 18 |
| **Restricted products** | Certain products (supplements, weight loss) require additional certification |

**Required TikTok Disclosures:**
```
#ad (in caption)
Paid Partnership tag (platform feature)
"Individual results may vary."
```

### 3.3 Platform Comparison

| Requirement | Meta | TikTok |
|---|---|---|
| Medical disclaimer | Required | Required |
| Before/after allowed | With restrictions + disclosure | With restrictions + pre-approval |
| #ad disclosure | Recommended | Required (paid partnership tag) |
| Health claim substantiation | Required on file | Required on file |
| Age restrictions on wellness ads | Recommended | Required (18+) |
| Personal attributes in ad copy | Prohibited | Prohibited |
| Urgency/scarcity claims | Must be genuine | Must be genuine |

## 4. FTC Endorsement Guidelines

### 4.1 Core Principles

The FTC requires that endorsements and testimonials in advertising:

1. **Reflect honest opinions and actual experience** — no fabricated testimonials
2. **Disclose material connections** — if an endorser is paid, given free product, or has a business relationship, this MUST be disclosed
3. **Disclosures must be clear and conspicuous** — visible and understandable to the average consumer
4. **No misleading claims** — endorsers cannot make claims the advertiser cannot substantiate
5. **Typicality disclosure** — if results shown are not typical, this must be clearly stated

### 4.2 Agent Rules for Endorsements

When an agent generates or reviews endorsement content:

```
ENDORSEMENT COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST
[ ] Is the endorser's connection to the brand disclosed? (#ad, paid partnership, "sponsored")
[ ] Does the endorsement reflect a genuine opinion (not fabricated)?
[ ] Are any results shown clearly marked as "individual results may vary"?
[ ] Is the disclosure clear and conspicuous (not buried in hashtags)?
[ ] Does the endorser make any claim the brand cannot substantiate?
[ ] If children are involved, are additional COPPA considerations met?

If ANY answer is NO → content is non-compliant. Revise or escalate.
```

### 4.3 Disclosure Placement

| Platform | Disclosure Format | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | `#ad` or `#sponsored` + paid partnership tag | First line of caption, before "more" fold |
| TikTok | `#ad` + paid partnership tag | First line of caption, visible without expanding |
| Facebook | `#ad` or `#sponsored` | First line of post text |
| YouTube | "This video contains paid promotion" + description disclosure | Platform disclosure + description |
| Blog/website | "Sponsored" or "Advertisement" label | Before the endorsement content, clearly visible |

## 5. ASA (UK) Rules

### 5.1 ASA / CAP Code Requirements

The Advertising Standards Authority (UK) enforces the CAP Code for non-broadcast advertising:

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Substantiation** | All claims must be supported by adequate evidence before publication |
| **Truthfulness** | Ads must not mislead consumers |
| **Health claims** | Must be authorized on the GB Register of nutrition and health claims (for food/supplements) |
| **Cosmetic claims** | Must not imply medicinal benefits; "cosmetic" claims only |
| **Before/after** | Must not mislead; results must be achievable and typical |
| **Endorsements** | Must clearly disclose paid relationships; celebrity endorsements must be genuine |
| **Children** | Ads must not exploit children's credulity or lack of experience |
| **Comparisons** | Must be fair, verifiable, and not misleading |

### 5.2 UK-Specific Disclaimers

For UK-market advertising, include as applicable:

```
"Individual results may vary."
"Sponsored content" (for paid placements)
"#ad" (for social media)
"This is a cosmetic product and is not intended to treat, cure, or prevent any medical condition."
```

## 6. Required Disclaimers

### 6.1 Standard Disclaimer Library

Agents must include the appropriate disclaimer(s) based on content type:

| Content Type | Required Disclaimer |
|---|---|
| Health/beauty product ad | "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." |
| Before/after imagery | "Individual results may vary. Results not guaranteed." |
| Paid endorsement / influencer | "#ad" + platform paid partnership tag |
| Testimonial | "Individual results may vary. This testimonial reflects personal experience." |
| Weight management product | "To be used in conjunction with a balanced diet and exercise." |
| Supplement product (US) | "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA." |
| UK cosmetic ad | "This is a cosmetic product and is not intended to treat medical conditions." |
| Limited-time offer | "Offer valid while supplies last. Terms and conditions apply." |

### 6.2 Disclaimer Placement Rules

1. Disclaimers must be **visible without requiring user action** (no "see more" to find disclaimers)
2. Disclaimers must be in **readable font size** — not micro-text
3. Disclaimers must appear **on the same screen** as the claim
4. For video content, disclaimers must be **on-screen for at least 3 seconds** and readable
5. For audio content, disclaimers must be **spoken clearly**

## 7. Influencer Disclosure Requirements

### 7.1 When Disclosure Is Required

Disclosure is required when there is a **material connection** between the influencer and the brand:

| Connection Type | Disclosure Required? | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Paid sponsorship | **Yes** | Brand pays influencer for post |
| Free product | **Yes** | Brand sends free product for review |
| Affiliate link | **Yes** | Influencer earns commission on clicks |
| Business relationship | **Yes** | Influencer is an employee or investor |
| Genuine unpaid review | No | Influencer bought product themselves |

### 7.2 Required Disclosure Tags

| Platform | Required Tags | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | `#ad` or `#sponsored` + Paid Partnership tag | `#ad` in first line + platform tag |
| TikTok | `#ad` + Paid Partnership tag | `#ad` in first line + platform tag |
| Facebook | `#ad` or `#sponsored` | First line of post |
| YouTube | Paid promotion disclosure + description | Platform disclosure + `#ad` in description |
| Twitter/X | `#ad` | Beginning of tweet |

### 7.3 Prohibited Influencer Practices

Agents must flag and escalate the following:

- **Hidden disclosures** — burying #ad in a sea of hashtags
- **Ambiguous tags** — using #sp, #collab, or #partner instead of #ad (not clear enough per FTC)
- **Fabricated testimonials** — creating fake reviews or testimonials
- **Undisclosed material connection** — influencer posts about brand without disclosing payment/free product
- **Misleading results** — influencer shows results that are not typical without disclosure

## 8. Agent Advertising Compliance Checklist

All agent-generated advertising content must pass this checklist before being submitted as a draft:

```
ADVERTISING COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

Medical Claims:
[ ] No claims of curing, treating, diagnosing, or preventing disease
[ ] No implied pharmaceutical effects
[ ] No "completely safe" language

Guarantees:
[ ] No guaranteed results
[ ] No specific timeframe promises
[ ] No "100% effective" or universal claims

Before/After:
[ ] No before/after without "Individual results may vary" disclaimer
[ ] No digitally altered before/after imagery
[ ] Compliance team has approved imagery

Platform Rules:
[ ] Meta: No personal attributes in ad copy
[ ] Meta: Health disclaimer included
[ ] TikTok: #ad + paid partnership tag (if influencer content)
[ ] TikTok: No targeting users under 18 for wellness ads
[ ] Appropriate platform disclaimers included

Endorsements:
[ ] Material connections disclosed
[ ] #ad or #sponsored in first line
[ ] Testimonials reflect genuine experience
[ ] Non-typical results disclosed

Regional:
[ ] US: FDA disclaimer for supplements (if applicable)
[ ] UK: ASA-compliant (no medicinal claims for cosmetics)
[ ] EU: GDPR-compliant data handling in ad targeting

If ANY answer is NO → content is non-compliant. Revise or escalate.
```

## 9. Escalation Triggers

The following advertising content must be escalated to human review:

| Trigger | Priority | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Content contains potential medical claim | P2 | Flag, do not publish, escalate to compliance |
| Before/after imagery not yet approved | P2 | Hold, escalate for compliance review |
| Influencer disclosure missing or unclear | P2 | Flag, request correction, escalate |
| Platform-specific rule uncertainty | P3 | Hold, escalate for compliance guidance |
| Competitor comparison claim | P2 | Escalate for legal review |
| Weight loss or anti-aging content | P2 | Escalate for compliance review |
